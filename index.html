Review of Data

London East Asia Film Festival (LEAFF) 2017

Avision Ho

2018-09-11

Executive Summary

From the data analysed in this report, the key findings are:

The curated film programme at LEAFF 2017 catered to a mainstream and large audience, and more particularly, being of most interest to the key age groups of 20 - 30.

The marketing strategy attracted a good range of people from different backgrounds who were new to the festival and returned to the festival from previous years.

The additional events such as Q&A sessions and workshops successfully engaged audiences enough to develop their interest and intrigue in film beyond just watching film.

The overall delivery of the entire festival was effective in receiving positive reviews from attendees.

Lastly, the overall festival experience was of such quality that 92% of survey respondents will return again in the future.

Introduction

LEAFF aims to champion the growing collaboration in East Asian filmmaking with a philosophy that marks a shift in the cinematic landscape of East Asia, and moves away from cultural and cinematic borders. Our vision is to bring a much wider, eclectic, and diverse programme of films from over 10 countries to show the richness and diversity of the region and its people. We want to draw people in with the familiar, and offer opportunities to see something new.

— Hyejung Jeon, Festival Director

This paper analyses the data collected from the London East Asia Film Festival (LEAFF) 2017. It does so with the intention of providing a summary of how successful the event was, and to provide a benchmark for future editions of LEAFF.

Section 1: General Information

This section presents a general high-level overview of the film shown and where they were screened.

Section 1.1: Films Shown and Locations

  • For the 2017 festival a wide range of films from different nations that included South Korea, Japan, Hong Kong, Vietnam, and the Phillippines were shown.1 Full programme here These included The Fortress (2017), Love Off The Cuff (2017), V.I.P. (2017), Bhoy Instik (2017), End of April (2016).

  • The screenings for these films took place in a variety of locations, mostly in London, but with a few in other regions in the UK. These included Odeon Leicester Square, Odeon Tottenham Court Road, Empire Haymarket, Sheffield Showroom.

Section 1.2: Accessibility

  • For each of these film screenings, all of them were an accessibility screening. Of those that were an accessbility screening, all of them had descriptive subtitles and/or were captioned.

Section 1.3: Additional Events

  • Beyond showing films, LEAFF 2017 offered audiences the chance to be more involved with films and cinema by hosting a series of events to accompany some of the festival’s film screenings. These events involved sessions such as Q&A, Introduction, Panel Discussion, Talk.

Section 1: Summary

Given the range of films screened, the way these screenings met accessibility requirements, and the additional events that were hosted during the festival to complement the films, then LEAFF 2017 offered all audiences an eclectic range of activities and films to enjoy.

Section 2: Summary

Given the relatively equal split between genders, the popularity of the festival with younger age groups, and the festival’s ability to attract both East Asian and non-East Asian ethnic groups, LEAFF 2017 was effective in catering to the key audience group of younger adults equally between genders, and within this subgroup, attracting a wide range of people from different ehtnic backgrounds.
